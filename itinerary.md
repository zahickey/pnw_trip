## Notes by leg

**Sierra Nevada (stops 2–5):** Joshua Tree → Yosemite is the longest early push (320 mi). Tuolumne Meadows camping should be booked as early as possible; late-July availability will be tight.

**Oregon/Washington (stops 6–12):** This is the most stop-dense leg — a new city or park almost every day. If the pace feels rushed once you're on the road, the easiest days to extend are Portland, Seattle, or Crater Lake; Squamish and the parks are worth protecting.

**Squamish (stop 13):** The trip's climbing anchor — 4 nights, the most of any stop besides Ketchikan.

**BC interior → Yukon-adjacent (stops 14–18):** Long transit days connecting Squamish to the Yellowhead Highway corridor. Jasper sits directly on Highway 16, so no backtracking is needed to continue west.

**Hyder, AK (stop 19):** The only Alaska stop reached purely by road — no ferry, no booking risk. Worth treating as a real highlight, not just a pass-through.

**Ferry to Ketchikan (stops 20–21):** Book the AMHS Prince Rupert–Ketchikan sailing as soon as dates are firm — this route has seen reduced and seasonal service in recent years, sometimes as few as ~2 sailings/month.
