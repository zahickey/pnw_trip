## Notes by leg

**Sierra Nevada, beginner climbing block (stops 2–3):** Joshua Tree gets a dedicated top-rope day at Trashcan Rock before the long push to Yosemite (320 mi). Tuolumne Meadows is now 4 nights to fit a 3-day progression — top-rope, easy multi-pitch, then an optional Cathedral Peak summit day. Book Tuolumne camping as early as possible; late-July/early-August availability will be tight, especially for a 4-night block. Have a backup plan (Inyo NF dispersed camping nearby).

**Oregon/Washington (stops 6–12):** This is the most stop-dense leg — a new city or park almost every day, now leaning harder into the marquee hikes at each (Skyline Trail at Rainier, Hurricane Hill at Olympic, Maple Pass at North Cascades). If the pace feels rushed once you're on the road, the easiest days to extend are Portland, Seattle, or Crater Lake; Squamish and the parks are worth protecting.

**Squamish (stop 13):** The trip's climbing anchor — 4 nights, tied with Yosemite for the longest stop.

**BC interior → Cassiar Highway (stops 14–18):** Long transit days connecting Squamish to the Yellowhead Highway corridor, then north on the Cassiar to Stewart/Hyder. Jasper sits directly on Highway 16, so no backtracking is needed to continue west.

**Hyder, AK (stop 19) — trip end:** The only Alaska stop reached purely by road — no ferry, no booking risk. Two nights here now, so there's real time for Salmon Glacier and Fish Creek without rushing back out. Confirm lodging/camping availability in Hyder or across the border in Stewart, BC before departure.
