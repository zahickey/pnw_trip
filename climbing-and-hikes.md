# Climbing & Hikes

Route-by-route detail on the two things this trip is actually for: getting on rock, and walking through beautiful places.

Climbing note: this itinerary is built around a beginner climber traveling with an experienced partner. The Joshua Tree and Yosemite stops are deliberately structured as a progression — top-rope fundamentals first, then an easy multi-pitch, with a harder optional day only once the basics feel solid.

## Climbing stops

### Joshua Tree National Park — beginner top-rope day
One dedicated day at **Trashcan Rock**, one of the park's most popular beginner areas — anchors are walk-up (no leading required to set up top-ropes), and the approach is short. Recommended routes:
- **Loose Lady (5.6)** — good first route, straightforward face moves
- **Double Cross (5.7)** — a step up in difficulty, still very top-rope friendly
- **The Eye (5.6)** — a third lap if energy allows

Good for shaking off the drive from San Diego and dialing in belay/rappel basics before the bigger granite at Yosemite.

### Yosemite — Tuolumne Meadows — 3-day progression
High-country granite domes, more moderate grades and less crowding than the Valley. Structured across 3 days:
- **Day 1 — Puppy Dome:** low-angle granite apron a few minutes off Tioga Rd, easy walk-off descent. Ideal first day on real rock — multiple toprope lines of varying difficulty on the same slab.
- **Day 2 — Daff Dome, Bunny Slopes (5.6, 4 pitches):** a classic, low-commitment first multi-pitch. Easy slab climbing with good pro and straightforward belay ledges.
- **Day 3 — optional step-up, Cathedral Peak, SW Face (5.6, 5 pitches):** iconic alpine summit route with a short approach hike; have the experienced partner lead. If it doesn't feel like the right day for it, swap in the Cathedral Lakes hike (7 mi RT) instead — no pressure to climb every day.

### Smith Rock (Bend, OR)
Mostly bolted sport climbing on volcanic tuff — considered a birthplace of American sport climbing. Misery Ridge Trail loops over the top of the formations for a non-climbing view of the whole park.

### Squamish, BC — the trip's anchor stop
World-class trad and multi-pitch granite. The Stawamus Chief is the centerpiece — long splitter cracks, big exposure, moderate-to-hard grades across multiple peaks (North, Centre, South Summits). The Chief Hike (non-technical) is a strong alternative or rest-day option with huge views over Howe Sound.

## National park hikes

### Joshua Tree NP
Ryan Mountain (3 mi RT) — the single best panoramic viewpoint in the park, great at sunset. Hidden Valley Nature Trail (1 mi loop) and Barker Dam Loop (1.3 mi, petroglyphs + reservoir) are both easy add-ons if there's extra time.

### Yosemite — Tuolumne Meadows
Cathedral Lakes (7 mi RT) — granite spires reflected in alpine lakes, one of the best hikes in the park. Dog Lake (2.8 mi RT, easy) makes a good lower-effort evening walk from the Lembert Dome trailhead.

### Crater Lake NP
Rim Drive (scenic drive, ~33 mi loop) hits nearly every overlook. Cleetwood Cove Trail is the only route down to the water — steep but short, check seasonal status before counting on it. Garfield Peak Trail (3.4 mi RT) gives the best rim panorama and is usually far less crowded.

### Mt. Rainier NP
Skyline Trail from Paradise (5.5 mi loop) — the classic wildflower-meadow loop with close-up glacier views, particularly vivid in late July/early August bloom. Naches Peak Loop is a good shorter alternative if time is tight.

### Olympic NP
Three distinct ecosystems in one park: Hurricane Ridge + Hurricane Hill Trail (3.2 mi RT, alpine), Hoh Rainforest (temperate rainforest, moss-draped), and Rialto Beach to Hole-in-the-Wall (coastal, tide pools and sea stacks). Worth the driving to link all three.

### North Cascades NP
Maple Pass Loop (7.2 mi) — consistently ranked one of the most scenic day hikes in Washington, big alpine payoff for a half-day effort. Diablo Lake Overlook is a five-minute stop for one of the most photographed views in the state (glacial-blue water).

### Banff NP
Lake Louise lakeshore walk, extended into the Plain of Six Glaciers teahouse hike for those who want more distance. Moraine Lake Rockpile is the classic quick-hit viewpoint — arrive early, both fill parking lots by mid-morning in summer. Johnston Canyon to the Ink Pots is an easy, well-trafficked trail to waterfalls, good for a lower-effort day.

### Jasper NP
The Icefields Parkway between Banff and Jasper is the headline drive — Peyto Lake and Bow Lake viewpoints, and the Athabasca Glacier where you can walk close to the ice. Wilcox Pass Trail gives arguably the best glacier views in the area without setting foot on the ice itself. Maligne Canyon for a quieter, shorter stop.

## Hyder, AK — trip end
Not a hiking destination in the traditional sense, but the drive up to Salmon Glacier is one of the best short drives on the whole trip — the world's largest glacier accessible by road. Fish Creek, just outside town, is a boardwalk platform for watching grizzlies fish salmon at close range. With two nights here now, there's room to do both without rushing.
