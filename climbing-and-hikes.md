# Climbing & Hikes

Route-by-route detail on the two things this trip is actually for: getting on rock, and walking through beautiful places.

## Climbing stops

### Joshua Tree National Park
Bouldering and easy-to-moderate trad on featured monzogranite. Hidden Valley is the classic warm-up area — short approaches, high concentration of routes. Good for shaking off the drive from San Diego.

### Yosemite — Tuolumne Meadows
High-country granite domes: Lembert Dome, Fairview Dome, Cathedral Peak. More moderate grades and less crowding than the Valley, with alpine scenery to match. Cathedral Peak's SE Buttress is a classic, moderate multi-pitch with a summit payoff.

### Smith Rock (Bend, OR)
Mostly bolted sport climbing on volcanic tuff — considered a birthplace of American sport climbing. Misery Ridge Trail loops over the top of the formations for a non-climbing view of the whole park.

### Squamish, BC — the trip's anchor stop
World-class trad and multi-pitch granite. The Stawamus Chief is the centerpiece — long splitter cracks, big exposure, moderate-to-hard grades across multiple peaks (North, Centre, South Summits). The Chief Hike (non-technical) is a strong alternative or rest-day option with huge views over Howe Sound.

## National park hikes

### Crater Lake NP
Rim Drive (scenic drive, ~33 mi loop) hits nearly every overlook. Cleetwood Cove Trail is the only route down to the water — steep but short, check seasonal status before counting on it.

### Mt. Rainier NP
Skyline Trail from Paradise — the classic wildflower-meadow loop with close-up glacier views, particularly vivid in late July/early August bloom.

### Olympic NP
Three distinct ecosystems in one park: Hurricane Ridge (alpine), Hoh Rainforest (temperate rainforest, moss-draped), and the coastal beaches (tide pools, sea stacks). Worth the driving to link all three.

### North Cascades NP
Maple Pass Loop or Cutthroat Pass — both give big alpine payoff for a half-day effort. Diablo Lake Overlook is a five-minute stop for one of the most photographed views in Washington (glacial-blue water).

### Banff NP
Lake Louise and Moraine Lake are the must-sees — arrive early, both fill parking lots by mid-morning in summer. Johnston Canyon is an easy, well-trafficked trail to waterfalls, good for a lower-effort day.

### Jasper NP
The Icefields Parkway between Banff and Jasper is the headline drive — Peyto Lake and Bow Lake viewpoints, and the Athabasca Glacier where you can walk close to the ice. Maligne Lake for a quieter, less-trafficked stop.

## Bonus: Hyder, AK
Not a hiking destination in the traditional sense, but the drive up to Salmon Glacier is one of the best short drives on the whole trip — the world's largest glacier accessible by road. Fish Creek, just outside town, is a boardwalk platform for watching grizzlies fish salmon at close range.

## Ketchikan, AK
Misty Fjords National Monument — reachable by floatplane or boat tour from Ketchikan — is the single best "beautiful place" payoff to end the trip on: sheer granite cliffs rising straight out of the water, waterfalls, old-growth rainforest.
