14K likes, 215 comments - thecozykansan on May 1, 2024: "ONE DAY in Washington’s Olympic National Park 👇🏻 SAVE for future reference!

Locations + More Info:
🌿 First off—a reminder to follow leave no trace principles while exploring the park to leave it better than you found it! Stay on marked trails & pack out what you pack in when hiking. 💚

🌊 Lake Crescent 
This glacially carved lake is full of deep, blue water and is a must-see on every trip to Olympic! If driving from Seattle, you’ll pass it on the main highway anyway, but definitely need to make a stop. I love to stop at the first viewpoint next to the welcome sign and then the pebbled beach & long dock next to Lake Crescent Lodge (which is also a great place to stay or grab a meal)! You can also hike to Devil’s Punchbowl on the northern side of the lake—a 2.4 mile RT paved path to a small cove of bright turquoise water that you can swim in during the summer months!

💦 Marymere Falls
This easy, 1.7 miles RT hike delves into the lush forest of Olympic, crossing multiple bridges, and ends at the stunning, 90-foot-tall Marymere Falls!

💦 Sol Duc Falls
The 1.6 miles RT hike to Sol Duc Falls is full of gorgeous green moss & ferns, small cascading falls, and towering trees. But the star of the show is absolutely Sol Duc Falls which splits into multiple channels as it cascades 48 feet to the canyon below.

🌲 Hoh Rainforest
This temperate rainforest is home to old growth trees up to a thousand years old as well as one of the quietest spots in the USA. My favorite hikes are the Hall of Mosses and Spruce Nature Trail which can be combined into a 2.1 mile loop hike!

🌊 Ruby Beach
The stunning, pebble-lined Ruby Beach is said to be one of the most gorgeous beaches on the entire Olympic Peninsula by many—myself included! This beach is accessed by a short trail from the parking lot and offers views of unique sea stacks and massive amounts of driftwood.
